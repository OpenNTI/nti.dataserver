Similarity Stretch
  1. Two geometric figures are similar if all of their corresponding angles are congruent and all of their corresponding sides are proportional. This means that the figures have the exact same shape but not necessarily the same size. For two triangles to be similar, it is sufficient to know that two pairs of corresponding angles are congruent.
