Collaborate with Your Community
UNDERSTANDING THE COMMUNITY PANEL

The “Community Panel” is located in the right side of your browser. It enables collaboration with others to personalize your learning experience. The “People” and “Groups” tabs within the “Community Panel” toggle the view between individuals in your community and your groups.

  • The People tab shows the status of individuals in your community. They may be listed as online, offline, or active.

  • The Groups tab shows which individuals are in each group.

  • “+” allows you to add new individuals to your community, create new groups, and add people to your groups.

  • Active shows which people have recently shared an Interaction with you.

  • Online shows which people are logged into NextThought.

  • Offline shows which people are not currently logged into NextThought.

Creating a new group

  1. Click the “+” icon on the right-hand side of the Community Panel.

  2. Enter a name for the new group in the “Add Groups” box.

  3. Click the “Add” button.

Adding an individual to an existing group

  1. Click the “+” icon on the right-hand side of the Community Panel.

  2. Type a person’s name into the “Add People” box.

  3. Select the appropriate person.

  4. Select an existing group (or groups).

  5. Click the “Finish” button to save the changes. Current members of the group can be found in the Groups tab within the Community Panel.

Deleting a group

  1. Click the “+” icon on the right-hand side of the Community Panel.

  2. Move the cursor over the group you want to delete.

  3. Click the “-” icon that appears. This deletes the group but does not remove the people in that group from your contacts.

SHARING INTERACTIONS
Sharing a Highlight

  1. Click an existing Highlight.

  2. Click “Share With...” to open the “Sharing Window.”

  3. Type in the names of individuals or groups into the “Search...” box and select the appropriate individuals or groups. You can also click the drop-down arrow to manually select groups.

  4. Click “Save.”

Sharing a Note or Whiteboard
Option 1—Sharing while adding a new Note

  1. Click the Sharing drop-down menu in the upper right-hand corner of the Note box.

  2. Select the appropriate groups, or select “Custom” to manually define your audience. NextThought saves this selection as your default sharing settings for future Interactions.

  3. Click “Save.”

Option 2—Sharing an existing Note

  1. Hover your cursor over an existing note and click the drop-down menu.

  2. Click the “Share” icon to open the Sharing Window.

  3. Type in the names of individuals or groups into the “Search...” box and select the appropriate individuals or groups. You can also click the drop-down arrow to manually select groups.

  4. Click “Save.”

Replying to a shared Note

Once a Note is shared, it will appear within the margin of users’ content. If it was recently shared, it will also appear within your Notifications.

  1. Hover your cursor over the desired shared Note in the left margin of your content.

  2. Click the “Reply” icon. The Note window will open with the original Note and all replies. You can comment on the original Note or one of the replies by hovering your cursor over the desired comment and clicking “Reply.”

  3. Type your reply and/or add a Whiteboard.

  4. Click “Save.”

STARTING A CHAT AND CREATING A GROUP CHAT

There are two options to begin a Chat: with an individual or with a Group.

Option 1—Sharting a Chat within a Note

  1. Hover your cursor over an existing shared Note and click the “Start a Chat” icon. A new Chat window will appears in the bottom right-hand corner of your browser. Your Chat includes anyone online who can see the original note.

Option 2—Starting a Chat outside of a Note
Begin a Chat with and individual

  1. Click on a person in the Community Panel who is online to start a Chat with that person. The Chat window appears in the bottom right-hand corner of the browser.

Begin a Chat with a Group

  1. Click the Chat icon located within the Group tab to start a Chat with the group. All members of that group currently online will be included in the Chat.
