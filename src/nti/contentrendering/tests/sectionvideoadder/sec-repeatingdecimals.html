We’ve seen that every fraction converts to either a finite decimal or an infinite repeating decimal. But what about decimals that are infinite but not repeating? These numbers do exist: they are called irrational numbers. (By contrast, any number that can be written as a fraction with integer numerator and denominator is called a rational number.)
