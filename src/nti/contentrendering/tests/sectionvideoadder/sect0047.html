Prealgebra : Ratios, Conversions, and Rates : Other Rates
7.6 Other Rates

Speed is just a special example of a rate. Whenever a quantity changes by a certain amount in a fixed unit of time, we have a rate. The idea of rate is very flexible and can be used in a lot of different situations.

Section Problems

  1. Jason can type at a rate of 40 words per minute. How long will it take him to type a word essay?

  2. A hose fills a swimming pool at a rate of 0.5 gallons per second. If the pool’s capacity is gallons, then how many hours does it take for the hose to completely fill an empty pool?

  3. Julie wants to give a 45-minute speech, and she speaks 120 words per minute. Her written notes contain 500 words per page. How many pages should she prepare?

  4. Rajiv’s car has tires that have circumference 75 inches. (The circumference of a tire is the distance around the outside of the tire.) How many revolutions will his tires make if Rajiv drives to a store that is mile away? (Assume that the drive is completely straight, and recall that 1 mile is feet.)

  5. If 5 woodchucks could chuck 50 cords of wood in 4 days, then how many cords of wood could 7 woodchucks chuck in 6 days?

  6. Tom can paint Mr. Thatcher’s fence in 6 hours, while Huck can paint Mr. Thatcher’s fence in 5 hours. If they work together, then how long will it take them to paint the fence?

End section problems

Just as with speed, the use of the word “per” is likely a signal that we are working with a rate. Also, just as with speed, we can use the units to our advantage when solving problems involving rates.

Unit conversions are just another type of rate. We can work with rates much like we do conversion factors. In particular, use them in mathematical expressions so that the units cancel in the way that you want.

Julie wants to give a 45-minute speech, and she speaks 120 words per minute. Her written notes contain 500 words per page. How many pages should she prepare?

Solution for Problem 30:

We have a couple of different rates here: both the words per minute and the words per page. We could do this as a two-step problem, or use both rates at once.

Method 1: Count words first, then count pages. First, we compute how many words should be in the speech. Julie wants to talk for 45 minutes and she’ll use 120 words per minute, so she will speak words. Then, we compute the number of pages: 5400 words at 500 words per page will require pages.

Exercises

  1. Casey has to build a 100-foot-long fence. If it takes her 15 minutes to build 1 foot of the fence, then how many hours will it take her to complete the fence?

  2. Phil can type a page of his new novel in 20 minutes. If he writes for 8 hours, then how many pages will he type?

  3. A kangaroo chases a rabbit that starts 150 feet ahead of the kangaroo. For every 12-foot leap of the kangaroo, the rabbit makes a 7-foot leap. How many leaps will the kangaroo have to make to catch up to the rabbit if the two animals always leap at the same time? (Source: MOEMS)

  6. A twelve-hour clock loses 1 minute every hour. Suppose it shows the correct time now. What is the least number of hours from now when it will again show the correct time? (Source: MOEMS)

  7. Homer began peeling a pile of 44 potatoes at the rate of 3 potatoes per minute. Four minutes later, Christen joined him and peeled at the rate of 5 potatoes per minute. When they finished, how many potatoes had Christen peeled? (Source: AMC 8)

  8. The cold-water faucet of a bath tub can fill the tub in 15 minutes. The drain, when opened, can empty the full tub in 20 minutes. Suppose the tub is empty and the faucet and drain are both opened at the same time. How long will it take to fill the tub? (Source: MOEMS)

  9. Roger can shovel his family’s driveway in 1 hour. His older sister Alexis can shovel the driveway in hour. If they work together, then how long will it take them to shovel the driveway?

  10. Three water pipes are used to fill a swimming pool.
