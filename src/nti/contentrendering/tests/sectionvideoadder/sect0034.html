5.6 Summary

In this chapter, we learned how to solve one-variable linear equations. By “solving” an equation, we mean finding all values of the variable for which the equation is true. The “one-variable” in “one-variable linear equation” means that only one variable appears in the equation, though it may appear multiple times. The “linear” means that every term in the equation is a constant term or is a constant times the first power of the variable.

We can often solve word problems by turning them into linear equations.

The key to solving word problems is converting the words into the language of mathematics. To do so, assign a variable to be a quantity you seek. Then, try to build an equation to solve for that variable.

When solving a word problem, define your variable clearly and use it exactly as you’ve defined it.
