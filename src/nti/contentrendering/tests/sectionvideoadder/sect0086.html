  2. In a group of 25 girls, 8 joined the track team, 13 joined the math team, and 6 joined both teams. How many of the girls did not join either team? (Source: MOEMS)

  3. There are 40 students in Mrs. Rusczyk’s first grade class. If there are three times as many students with blond hair as with blue eyes, 3 students with blond hair and blue eyes, and 15 students with neither blond hair nor blue eyes, how many students have blue eyes?

  4. You have 4 shirts, 3 pairs of pants, and 6 hats. How many outfits can you make consisting of one shirt, one pair of pants, and one hat?

  5. A haunted house has six windows. In how many ways can Georgie the Ghost enter the house by one window and leave by a different window? (Source: AMC 8)

  6. How many 3-letter combinations can be formed if the first letter must be a vowel (A, E, I, O, or U), and the second letter must be different from the third letter?

  7. How many 5-digit numbers have the second digit odd and the fifth digit at least four times the second digit?

  8. Pat Peano has plenty of 0’s, 1’s, 3’s, 4’s, 5’s, 6’s, 7’s, 8’s, and 9’s, but he has only twenty-two 2’s. How far can he number the pages of his scrapbook with these digits? (Source: AMC 8)

  9. How many positive, even three-digit numbers exist such that the sum of the hundreds digit and the tens digit equals the units digit? (Source: MATHCOUNTS)

  10. A baseball league has nine teams. During the season, each of the nine teams plays exactly three games with each of the other teams. What is the total number of games played? (Source: MOEMS)

  11. Tyler has entered a buffet line in which he chooses one kind of meat, two different vegetables, and one dessert from the selections below. If the order of food items is not important, how many different meals might he choose?
    Meat: beef, chicken, pork
    Vegetables: baked beans, corn, potatoes, tomatoes
    Dessert: brownies, chocolate cake, chocolate pudding, ice cream
    (Source: AMC 8)

  12. A singles tournament had six players. Each player played every other player only once, with no ties. If Helen won 4 games, Ines won 3 games, Janet won 2 games, Kendra won 2 games, and Lara won 2 games, how many games did Monica win? (Source: AMC 8)

  15. A box contains 5 white balls and 6 black balls.

    1. A ball is drawn out of the box at random. What is the probability that the ball is white?
