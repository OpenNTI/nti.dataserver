FSA Reverse Stress Test Recommendations

This module is based on FSA Reverse Stress Testing recommendations. The FSA has been a leader in mandating RST as a core risk management tool for financial institutions, and has published helpful recommendations on stress testing best practices (e.g., PS09/20).

FSA recommends viewing key dimensions of RST along three axes, which represent important risk management polarities:

  1. External/Internal,

  2. Rapid/Protracted,

  3. Idiosynchratic/Marketwide.

Another core recommendation is to consider a feedback loop of Causes, Consequences, and Impacts for stress scenarios. We review two FSA case studies to illustrate this cycle (bank and asset manager).

We briefly discuss the recommended outputs of stress scenarios (i.e., concentration risk analysis, risk appetite & limits, triggers & monitoring). Finally, these scenarios should not be made public, but rather used for internal risk management purposes and regulatory submission.

Review Questions
Case Study: Major UK Bank

It’s August 2012. Financial scandals seem to be emerging on a weekly basis, from LIBOR to JPM’s “London Whale” CDS fiasco. The world economy seems to be slowing, and the EURO crisis is anything but resolved. You are the risk manager of a major UK Bank, which is also under investigation for LIBOR manipulation. You have a diversified global corporate and retail banking business, with significant growth in Emerging Markets, especially Asia. Capital ratios and liquidity profile are strong, although you do rely significantly on the REPO market for short term financing and need to refinance debt in early 2013. Your loan portfolio is concentrated in highly rated blue chip companies, and your liquid assets are invested mainly in European government debt.

  1. As a first step, what areas will you focus on in reverse stress testing?

  2. Show a Cause / Consequence / Impact spiral that could lead to significant impairment.
