Key Risk Reporting Limitations

We also discuss a useful list of market and credit risk reporting limitations that GS highlights in the following slide.

Amongst the important ones financial institutions faced during the recent GFC were crowded trades, illiquidity, basis risk, and tail risk.

  • From a systemic perspective, subprime securities had become a crowded trade, and as that trade was unwound, liquidity risk infected most other risky asset classes, from other MBS securities to broader credit markets, Money Market instruments, and equities.

  • Many investors saw that their actual risks were much higher than perceived, especially as basis risk escalated. For example, significant divergence between cash credit spreads and CDS spreads caused large losses in supposedly hedged bank lending portfolios.

  • The net results of these tail risks aggregating across the financial system was a systemic tail event.

It’s also worthwhile highlighting the Wrong Way Risk, which refers to a positive correlation between credit exposure and counterparty credit risk. For example, if a Korean bank buys Korean Won(KRW) vs USD FX forward, credit exposure grows if KRW falls. Furthermore, the Korean bank’s creditworthiness would deteriorate under KRW devaluation. The net result of such a scenario is escalating credit exposure with escalating default risk.
