Reverse Stress Testing Introduction

This chapter introduces Reverse Stress Testing (RST). The objective of process of RST is to gain insights on enterprise vulnerabilities by identifying scenarios that could result in unacceptably large losses. We start with a brief history, and include a JPMorgan case study and regulatory endorsements. We consider both qualitative approaches, for example as endorsed by the FSA, as well as quantitative approaches to RST.

The process of RST is straightforward:

  1. Specify threshold loss that would result in serious impairment (e.g., loss of $1bn+)

  2. Search for credible scenarios that could lead to such a loss, based on current portfolio / business.

To generate extreme enterprise scenarios, it’s useful to first consider key vulnerabilities, such as liquidity risk, concentration risk, and reputation risk.
