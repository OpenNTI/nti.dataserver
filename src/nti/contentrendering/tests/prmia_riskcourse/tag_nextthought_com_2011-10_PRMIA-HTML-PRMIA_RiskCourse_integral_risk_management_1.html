Integral Risk Management

True but Partial

We start with the “unresolved” historical tension in risk management between subjective and quantitative perspectives of risk. Both perspectives have validity, but are incomplete in isolation (i.e., true but partial). This is a classic polarity. The answer is not one or the other, but a cycle that involves both perspectives.

The Integral Framework

"The word integral means comprehensive, inclusive, non-marginalizing, embracing. Integral approaches to any field attempt to be exactly that: to include as many perspectives, styles, and methodologies as possible within a coherent view of the topic. In a certain sense, integral approaches are “meta-paradigms,” or ways to draw together an already existing number of separate paradigms into an interrelated network of approaches that are mutually enriching." - Ken Wilber (2003)

We will apply some of the core principles of philosopher Ken Wilber’s Integral Framework to risk management. The foundation of his framework considers Four Quadrants which represent key interdependent perspectives driven by the following polarities:

  1. Subjective Interior vs Objective Exterior

  2. Individual vs Collective

For more background on the universal Integral framework please see AN OVERVIEW OF INTEGRAL THEORY.

The slide below applies Wilber’s Integral framework to risk management.

  1. Starting in the upper right quadrant, we need good risk models and analytics (individual objective).

  2. But stand alone spreadsheets are of not much use if used in isolation; they need to be integrated into systems and governance structures (collective objective). This is the objective half of risk management.

  3. But we also need to consider subjective dimensions of risk management (left quadrants). First, we need to have professionals with integrity and expertise (individual subjective).

  4. But even the best risk managers with the best tools can not help an organization if a culture of risk management is lacking (collective subjective).

Importance of Risk Culture

Culture is at the very foundation of any organization, and represents its shared vision and values.

Multiple Lines of Intelligence

Another important dimension of Wilber’s All Quadrant All Levels And Lines (AQAL) model relates to multiple-intelligences, as popularized by Howard Gardner. Organizations are enriched through diverse teams which represent multiple-intelligences. Leadership teams which are imbalanced have major blind spots, and are less likely to thrive in the long run.

Connecting the Dots

Review Questions
Case Study: InX

As a risk consultant you’re asked to review the risk management practices of a large insurance company InX. InX prides itself on sophisticated actuarial tail risk models, which estimate their capital ratios at 99.98% confidence to target a AAA rating. InX primarily uses these models to drive their investment decisions. They have recently greatly expanded their structured credit portfolio, which they believe offer an attractive risk adjusted reward when one compares the market spread to their capital models.

Senior managers of the organization are experienced insurance professionals with a long history at InX. You notice that meetings are highly structured with pre-defined agendas, and that discussions are dominated by senior professionals. There is low tolerance for dissention, as well as for experimentation and failure. The mindset around risk management is compliance focused.

When it comes to stress testing, InX shows you their pre-defined scenarios, which are based on worst case historical movements. When you ask them about hypothetical stress tests, they dismiss this as “subjective,” and explain that the only objective way to stress test is using historical observations.

Discussion:

  1. Peer reviewed submission: What would you recommend to your client in terms of improving their organizational risk management? (200 words max)

  2. Peer review submission: How would you improve the stress testing process? How would you convince management about the usefulness of hypothetical stress tests and encourage their use? (200 words max)
