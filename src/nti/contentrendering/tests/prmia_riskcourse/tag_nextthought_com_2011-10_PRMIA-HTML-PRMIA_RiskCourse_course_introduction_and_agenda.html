Course Introduction and Agenda

We introduce the course by emphasizing the importance of risk management for sustaining civilization. We now face unprecedented systemic risks, with highly coupled financial, economic and ecological systems which could cascade into extreme tail events. The recent Global Financial Crisis has yielded expensive lessons about the dangers of reactive approaches to risk management. “Cleaning up the mess afterwards” is not a viable strategy.

To successfully navigate systemic risk, we must identify and manage emerging risks before irreversible tipping points are crossed. This course presents an integrated framework for risk management, with a focus on stress testing and early warning. We will draw on many interdisciplinary lessons from fields such as epidemiology, complexity, and biology. We view risk management holistically, as both art and science.

To succeed, we must go beyond measuring risk to managing it. We’ll draw lessons from an astounding collection of historical risk failures and successes. We’ll introduce polarity management as a core practice. We will apply developmental research to describe key developmental stages, from reactive to tactical to integrated risk management. And we will apply philosopher Ken Wilber’s All Quadrant All Levels (AQAL) model to propose an integrated framework for risk management.

Finally, we hope to leverage the power of social learning through the NextThought platform, and to encourage risk professionals to discuss current topics and foster lasting social connections.
