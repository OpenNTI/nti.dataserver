Reverse Stress Testing Using StressGrades™

This section briefly illustrates how StressGrades could be used for Reverse Stress Testing. StressGrades can help with two main objectives:

  1. Identify archetypal reverse scenarios, and

  2. Monitor escalating risks and prioritize focus

In general, Monte Carlo or historical simulation serve well for Reverse Stress Testing. After defining a key loss level (e.g., -2%), we could simply take all scenarios that exceed this loss level. This could, however, leave us with an overwhelming amount of scenarios, many of which are likely to be repetitive (e.g., -2% SPY, -2.1% SPY, etc.). We therefore need to group similar scenarios and derive a practical archetypal scenario monitor. This can be accomplished by grouping highly correlated scenarios and using the most likely scenario (i.e., lowest DStress or highest PStress). We can can also visually identify clusters of scenarios that move together.

We illustrate this with a simple example of a multi-asset class portfolio by visually identifying DStress time series which are correlated.

StressGrades Summary

StressGrades complement the existing stress testing process, and work on a macro and micro level.

From a macro perspective, StressGrades provides systemic early warning signals based on market signals.

From a micro perspective, StressGrades statistics can be integrated with portfolio specific risk factors, which can help risk managers quickly identify escalating vulnerabilities.

Review Exercise

You are a risk manager for a pension fund.

Your task is to Reverse Stress Test a portfolio based on exceeding a loss threshold of -10% in one month. Using 25 years of historical monthly return data, you identify 29 scenarios where you would have incurred losses of 10% or more.
