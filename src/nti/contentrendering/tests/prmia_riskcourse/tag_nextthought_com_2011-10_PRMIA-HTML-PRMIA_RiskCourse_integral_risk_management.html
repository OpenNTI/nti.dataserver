Integral Risk Management

Introduction

In this final module we present a framework for Integral Risk Management to bring together many of the perspectives we discussed earlier. Integral refers to “balanced, comprehensive, interconnected, and whole.” Challenged by complexity, corporate risk management has suffered from fragmentation, where interdependent risks are often considered in isolation. To see the big picture, we need to bring together these fragmented perspectives.

We present the material in three main segments:

  1. Managing Complexity discusses the key implications of complexity.

  2. Managing Polarities introduces a foundational risk management practice.

  3. Integral Risk Management identifies four key interdependent perspectives for achieving holistic risk management.

And then we wrap up with 4. Conclusions and Key Lessons.
