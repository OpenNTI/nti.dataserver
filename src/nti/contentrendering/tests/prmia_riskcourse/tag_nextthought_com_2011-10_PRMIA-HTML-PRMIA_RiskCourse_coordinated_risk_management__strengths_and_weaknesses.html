Coordinated Risk Management: Strengths and Weaknesses

Next we discuss how GS coordinates enterprise risk analysis across their five major divisions: Treasury, Legal, Compliance, Operations, and Credit.

We discuss which aspect of their risk management appears strongest and weakest, given what we observed during the GFC.

GS’s management of direct financial exposures has been strong:

  • superior risk adjusted investment and trading performance

  • avoided major credit losses

  • avoided major operational losses due to systems failures or rogue traders

However, not the same can be said for its management of reputation risk. In recent years, GS has suffered reputational damage from a number of controversies, its role in the subprime crisis (Abacus CDO, AIG bailout terms) to its structuring of transactions that masked Greece’s national debt levels. It appears that Goldman’s pursuit of short term profits has at times trumped its famous philosophy of being “long term greedy.”
