Visualisation technologies are an exciting frontier for risk management. With increased computational power and mobility, we can interactively engage with multi-dimensional risk perspectives as opposed static reports. This allows us to develop deeper systems level understanding about complex non-linear processes.

Risky Misperceptions: Don’t just look straight ahead...anticipate with peripheral vision

Overconfidence is a well proven cognitive bias. We tend to overestimate on the most likely scenarios, while we underestimate tail probabilities by a factor of 10 or more. This bias has been shown not only in the general public but also in financial analysts, economists, and senior executives.

We need to actively correct this bias through constant vigilance and peripheral vision... to prevent emergence from turning into an emergency.

Peripheral vision can be an important competitive advantage:
Most of the focus at most companies is on what’s directly ahead. The leaders lack “peripheral vision.” This can leave your company vulnerable to rivals who detect and act on ambiguous signals. 6 Habits of True Strategic Thinkers, Paul Schoemaker, Mar 20, 2012

Summary

Risk managers are faced with overwhelming volumes of information to process. The key challenge is to sift through continuously growing streams of data to better understand the evolution of systemic risk and potential portfolio vulnerabilities.

Instead of being limited by one time snapshots of risk, we can now interact with dynamic models and play animations about how major market factors evolve over time.

  • Heatmaps are useful for prioritizing attention, and can be viewed across time to detect anomalies and emerging risks.

  • Outlier analysis focuses attention on emerging signals, and is crucial for early warning. Plotting outlier trends over time can provide insights about systemic risk (e.g., broad outlier spikes and clustering).

  • Network graphs help us better understanding of dynamic interrelationships in complex systems.

We can also apply pattern recognition algorithms to improve our ability to detect unusual activities as well as highlight potential hidden portfolio concentrations which may emerge from the interaction of many different risk factors.
