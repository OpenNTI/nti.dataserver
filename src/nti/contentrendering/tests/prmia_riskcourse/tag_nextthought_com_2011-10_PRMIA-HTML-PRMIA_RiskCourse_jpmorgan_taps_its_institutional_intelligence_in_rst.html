JPMorgan Taps Its Institutional Intelligence in RST

Steve Thieke pioneered RST at JPM in the mid 1990s. Instead of asking “what losses would JPM suffer in the event of a major market correction,” he reversed the question into “what credible scenarios could lead to a major loss at JPM?” Given JPM’s enterprise VaR limit of $50mm, he considered this a useful threshold loss amount.

JPM then leveraged its organizational hierarchy through a web based system that required every risk taker to submit potential scenarios that could lead to a $50mm+ loss in their portfolios. Key scenarios were escalated up the firm’s hierarchy, from trading desk to business managers to CFO & CRO. The system leveraged the firm’s hierarchy for identifying concentrated risks across the firm. JPM’s CRO could then direct further analysis on key scenarios.
