A Macro Stress Summary

A brief review of best practices for macro stress testing.

We first start with a macro assessment of the overall environment. This includes considering the interdependent macroeconomic and financial landscape, in particular focusing on amplifiers such as capital flows, asset price movements, and leverage. We understand that under certain critical conditions (e.g., think forest fire or avalanche), even tiny perturbations can set off major phase transitions.

After identifying key systemic vulnerabilities, we can translate these into specific market scenarios which provide us with key variables to monitor.

We introduced the Adaptive Stress Library as a framework to integrate top-down scenario analysis with bottom-up early warning market signals. Top-down analysis focuses on hidden potential vulnerabilities, or fault lines. Bottom-up analysis closely monitors these fault lines for warning signals. Once an outlier is observed, we pay close attention to see if amplification mechanisms have been triggered. Endogenous crises are characterized by outliers and super-exponential changes, and ever greater waves of volatility.

Our best hope of mitigating systemic risk is to address structural risk is when it is still in its potential form. When potential risk becomes realized in a phase transition, our ability to exert control rapidly diminishes.

Therefore, the most important step in stress testing is the focus on structural vulnerabilities. We start on the macro systemic level. We then apply scenarios to test the vulnerabilities in our portfolios. To use an earthquake analogy, we start with identifying the major fault lines, and then we consider how we are positioned relative to these fault lines (i.e., how close are we to key fault lines, what kind of building have we constructed).

Bottom-up signals from the marketplace can help us assess the health of the financial system and, in particular, quickly focus on areas of instabilities (medical analogies would include measuring pulse and blood pressure). Given the many amplification mechanisms in finance, early detection is vital to prevent cascading risk. One home on fire can quickly engulf a city.

An Adaptive Stress Library is an ideal candidate for a risk commons. We all benefit early management of systemic risk. Thought leaders can help identify structural risks, while market signals harness the intelligence of the crowd.
