Macro Stress Testing

Introduction

Macro Stress testing discusses a range of topics: macro drivers of systemic risk, common debt crisis trajectories, theories on governance of complex systems, phase transitions, nonlinear interaction between amplifiers and dampeners, the impact of asymmetric central bank intervention, how Thought Leaders and Early Adopters help us build an Adaptive Stress Library, whether financial crises tend to be Black Swans or Dragon Kings, endogenous risk patterns, and using outlier statistics for early warning.

We present Macro Stress Testing in the following three modules:

  1. The first module focuses on archetypal drivers of financial crises, in particular the linkage between capital flows, credit, and asset prices.

  2. The second module introduces the Adaptive Stress Library framework, and considers how to pre-diagnose and measure systemic risk.

  3. The third module will focus on outliers as early warning signals.
