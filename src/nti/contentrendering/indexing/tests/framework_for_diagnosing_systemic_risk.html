Framework for Diagnosing Systemic Risk

Inspired by epidemiology and Geoffrey Moore’s “Crossing The Chasm” (1999), we propose an Adaptive Stress Library (ASL) which integrates scenario analysis with early warning. ASL consists of two building blocks:

  1. Thought Leaders (Innovators), who are the source credible macro scenarios (e.g., PIGS crisis, China Bust, Agflation, Iran War), which are then translated into key market movements.

  2. Early Adopters, who provide phase transition signals of emerging themes: outliers and escalating volatility which indicate that feedback mechanisms have been triggered.

ASL allows risk managers to first identify and analyze structural risks (macro), and then monitor key indicators (micro) and respond proactively to emerging scenarios.

As an example, key events in Nouriel Roubini’s “2012 Perfect Storm” macro scenario would be “stalling growth in the U.S., debt troubles in Europe, a slowdown in emerging markets, particularly China, and military conflict in Iran.” After translating this qualitative scenario into key market movements, risk managers can assess potential portfolio impacts and decide on a strategy. This could include buying put option protection to mitigate unacceptable losses, as well as pre-determining thresholds at which markets could offer potential investment opportunities. For example, during the 2008 subprime crisis GMO, which had been bearish on US Equities, determined their projected worst case scenario for equities and then set levels at which US equities would provide value to pre-determined their investment strategy (e.g., buy 10% shares if S&P drops to 800, buy additional 15% shares at 700, buy additional 20% shares at 600, etc.).

Macro and micro perspectives continually inform each other in an intelligent feedback loop. Macro analysis explores structural risks and hones in on key factors to monitor. Bottom-up micro outliers provide early warning about which macro fault lines show vulnerability.
